How Do I

A website to help colleagues in food stores find out how to do tasks in their jobs.

We wanted to give back time to colleagues in our food stores, we undertook a discovery to look at ways to make stores more efficient.

There were around 600 policies, processes and procedures for colleagues to look up, know and follow.

They were stored on a mixture of Citrus (a third party system), the intranet, colleagues.coop.co.uk, and other systems.

We spoke to store colleagues and found that they don’t know where to go to get information about processes.

So they:

  • Spent time trying to find information on various systems
  • Asked a manager
  • Risked not complying
  • Called Operational Support Services (OSS)

This can lead to confusion, frustration, distrust, disempowerment and lack of productivity.

We believe making information about procedures easy to find and understand for both store managers and colleagues will:

  • Reduce time spent looking for it
  • Increase colleague empowerment
  • Improve standards in store
  • We’ll know this is true when we see fewer ‘how do I’ calls to OSS, fewer customer complaints, and more stores dealing with things in the right way.

We built a minimum viable product (MVP) which had:

  • A good search
  • Policies that are user-focused and written in plain English
  • Fewer policies based on research, statistics and evidence

We wanted to learn:

  • What the highest value and most risky policies and procedures are
  • About the implications of easier to find, easier to understand policies and procedures on colleague morale
  • Which policies and procedures are most used and which are not needed
  • How colleagues prefer to learn
  • How much information can we surface publicly
  • The most effective ways and channels to give information

With a minimum viable product built we continued to research and re-write content, each week we would put the latest designs and procedures in front of store colleagues, gaining feedback and iterating, improving as we went on.

As the content continued to grow we then launched an early version of ‘how do i’ to 10 ’test’ stores in Manchester so they could access it from the back office computer. We then launched to more stores in Surrey and Glasgow which brought the number of test stores up to 47.

We built in a feedback mechanism on each page of ‘how do i’ that allowed colleagues to send us feedback directly, this proved to be a really useful feature in two ways, one helping us improve the design and content but also it was giving colleagues direct input into how to improve ‘how do i' becoming more involded.

We visited the stores to see how they were getting on, mostly the feedback was positive and we continued to listen to our colleagues in store improving and again iterating ‘how do i’. We launched to more stores, including the stores of excellence, the content now was getting more and more substantial.

‘How do i’ is now used in all our food stores and we will continue to learn and improve on it to help our food colleagues.
